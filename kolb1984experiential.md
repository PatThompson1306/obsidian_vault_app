#### Summary
Foundational text establishing experiential learning theory, positing that experience serves as the primary source of learning and development, drawing on work by Dewey, Piaget, and others to conceptualise learning as a cyclical process of knowledge construction through concrete experience, reflection, abstract conceptualisation, and active experimentation.

#### Key Points
Presents learning as an adaptive, constructivist process where knowledge is created through transformation of experience rather than passive transmission; introduces the experiential learning cycle and learning styles framework that has become widely influential across educational contexts, with over 36,000 citations reflecting its foundational impact on understanding how individuals learn from experience.
